## Reflection

### 1. What went well during this unit?

This unit went well because the team moved from broad ideas into concrete screens and data decisions without changing the core concept of **ReadWell**. Creating wireframes helped us agree on the main flow quickly: the library dashboard should be the home base, the quick-add experience should stay lightweight, and shelves plus reading groups deserve their own dedicated views. Once the page structure was clear, the ERD became much easier to discuss because each table directly supported a visible user action.

### 2. What were some challenges your group faced in this unit?

The biggest challenge was turning flexible feature ideas into a database shape that is still realistic for the time we have left in the course. We had to decide what belongs in the first version versus what should stay lightweight, especially around reading groups so we do not accidentally design a full social platform. Another challenge was making sure our wireframes stayed low-fidelity enough for planning while still being specific enough to guide development next unit.

### 3. What additional support will you need in upcoming units as you continue to work on your final project?

In the next unit, the most helpful support will be examples for setting up Express routes, Postgres migrations, and seed/reset workflows that match our ERD. We also want guidance on how to split frontend and backend work cleanly across the team so multiple people can build in parallel without stepping on each other. As we get closer to deployment, a clear Render checklist for environment variables, build commands, and connecting the React app to the API will be especially useful.
